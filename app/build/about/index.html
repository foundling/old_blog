Hi. I’m Alex Ramsdell, a JavaScripter and Python fanatic living in Seattle, Washington. This site exists as a place to articulate my interests in:

  • software and web development
  • Linux
  • philosophy
  • other interesting things
  • fun stuff, like my musical projects, photos of iceland and programming guides.
  • graphic design

The blog part of this site runs with the help of the following tools: Python, MongoDB, PyMongo, Flask, HTML, CSS, Sass, Nginx, gunicorn, CentOS and DigitalOcean. The programming guides are ... will be ... written in Angular in a text-book-y fashion.

These are in the works, due in some time in the form of web text-books, in Angular probably. The teaching in these books is humbly informed by my experience as a Linux sysadmin and a community programming instructor in Massachusetts. My students there taught me a great deal about the flexibility and communication skills necessary to help someone learn something in depth. I hope that someone might find one of these guides when they need it, as I found the GNU C tutorial and pointers were then clarified.

Design

Just one important person to mention here: H.N. Werkman. He was a very passionate and lonely Dutch printmaker who never really found the proper artistic community nor financial success and was eventually killed by the Nazis. He did amazing, groundbreaking things with type, and is a permanent member of my hall of fame. There isn't too much written about him in English, but the book H.N Werkman by Alston W. Purvis is a great, accessible introduction and basic history of his artistic life.
