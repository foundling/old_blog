About Me

I'm Alex Ramsdell, a software developer in Seattle. I'm interested in building edible and accessible user interfaces for the web, progressive web apps, web instruments and useful command-line tools.

My trajectory into softwareland has mostly been a self-taught affair (althought this may change soon) and I consider that a strength. I like constraints, I throw away a lot of code, and I live to refactor. These days I'm feeling the pull of the functional approach to software construction, as I keep discovering interesting and useful applications for higher-order functions.

Why No Code Highlighting?

I don't want to do it myself. I don't want to depend on a library that doesn't quite fit my design. And I like black code. Crockford is right to argue for this.

About This Site

I intend for this site to be a venue for focused articles on matters related to software. With that said, I've always had a difficult time maintaining any kind of firm boundary between software and the lessons and joys of life, so other topics may creep in.
