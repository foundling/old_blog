About Me

I'm Alex Ramsdell, a software developer in Seattle. I love building spare, edible and usable user interfaces for the web, offline-first 'progressive' web apps, brower-based instruments and command-line-based productivity tools.

My trajectory into softwareland has mostly been a self-taught affair (althought this may change soon) and I consider that a strength. I am always hungry to build and learn more things. These days I'm feeling the pull of the functional approach to software construction, as I keep discovering interesting and useful applications for higher-order functions.

About This Site

I intend for this site to be a venue for focused articles on matters related to software. With that said, I've always had a difficult time maintaining any kind of real boundary between software and the lessons and joys of life, so other topics may creep in. In any case, you'll find that the articles here will tend much more toward the timeless end of the spectrum rather than the keeping-up-with-the-dev-tools end of the spectrum.
